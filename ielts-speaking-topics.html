SPEAKING PART 1 — 4-5 PHÚT

Part 1: Câu hỏi quen thuộc

Giám khảo hỏi về bản thân, sở thích, thói quen. Trả lời 2-3 câu/câu hỏi. Đừng quá ngắn, đừng quá dài.

Work / Study

Q: Do you work or study?

Well, I'm currently pursuing a degree in Business Administration at a university in Ho Chi Minh City. I opted for this field because I've always been fascinated by how companies operate and grow. Down the line, I'm hoping to set up my own startup.

Q: What do you like about your studies?

What I find particularly rewarding is the hands-on projects we do. Rather than just memorizing theories, we get to apply concepts to real-world scenarios, which I find incredibly engaging.

Hometown

Q: Where are you from?

I hail from Da Nang, which is a vibrant coastal city in central Vietnam. It's renowned for its stunning beaches, particularly My Khe Beach, and boasts a fascinating blend of modern development and traditional charm.

Q: Has your hometown changed much?

Absolutely! It's undergone a dramatic transformation over the past decade. There's been a massive influx of tourism and investment, and the infrastructure has improved beyond recognition. That said, the locals have managed to preserve their warm hospitality.

Hobbies

Q: What do you do in your free time?

I'm really into photography — I tend to spend my weekends exploring different neighbourhoods and capturing street scenes. It's a great way to unwind and see the city from a fresh perspective. I also dabble in cooking Vietnamese cuisine.

Food

Q: What's your favourite food?

I'd have to say phở — it's the quintessential Vietnamese dish. I'm particularly fond of the beef version with a generous amount of fresh herbs. There's something incredibly comforting about having a warm bowl of phở on a rainy morning.

Weather

Q: What's the weather like in your country?

Vietnam has a tropical climate, so it's generally quite humid year-round. In the south, there are basically two seasons: the rainy season and the dry season. I have a soft spot for the cooler months from November to February — the weather is pleasantly mild and ideal for outdoor activities.

Technology

Q: Do you use technology a lot?

Without a doubt! Technology is pretty much woven into every aspect of my daily life. I rely heavily on my smartphone for everything from navigation to studying. That said, I make a conscious effort to disconnect in the evenings to maintain a healthy balance.

Health

Q: How do you stay healthy?

I try to lead an active lifestyle by jogging three times a week and being mindful of what I eat. I've also taken up yoga recently, which has been a game-changer for managing stress. I believe prevention is better than cure.

SPEAKING PART 2 — 1-2 PHÚT NÓI

Part 2: Cue Card

Được 1 phút chuẩn bị, nói 1-2 phút. Luôn bám sát 4 gạch đầu dòng trên thẻ.

Describe a Person
Describe a person who has had a great influence on you.
• Who this person is
• How you know them
• What they have done
• Why they influenced you

I'd like to talk about my high school English teacher, Ms. Lan. She was instrumental in shaping my academic journey. What set her apart was her unwavering dedication — she would go above and beyond to ensure every student understood the material. She ignited my passion for learning English, and it's largely thanks to her that I'm sitting here today taking the IELTS. She taught me that persistence is the key to success — a life lesson that has stuck with me ever since.

Describe a Place
Describe a place you visited that left a strong impression on you.
• Where it was
• When you went there
• What you did there
• Why it left a strong impression

One place that left an indelible mark on me was Hội An ancient town. I visited last spring and was utterly captivated by its charm. The streets were lined with centuries-old houses, and at night, thousands of lanterns cast a magical glow over the Thu Bồn River. What struck me most was how the town has preserved its heritage despite being a major tourist destination. It felt like stepping back in time.

Describe an Event
Describe a memorable event you attended.
• What the event was
• Where and when it took place
• Who was there
• Why it was memorable

The event that springs to mind is a music festival I attended two years ago in Đà Lạt. It was a three-day extravaganza featuring both local and international artists. The atmosphere was absolutely electric — thousands of people from all walks of life, united by their love of music. What made it truly unforgettable was a spontaneous jam session on the final night where the audience sang along in perfect harmony.

Describe an Object
Describe something you own that is important to you.
• What it is
• How you got it
• How often you use it
• Why it is important

I'd like to describe my well-worn journal that my grandmother gave me when I started university. I jot down my thoughts, goals, and reflections on a daily basis. It's become an indispensable part of my routine — a way to process my emotions and keep track of my personal growth. It holds tremendous sentimental value because it connects me to my grandmother.

SPEAKING PART 3 — 4-5 PHÚT

Part 3: Discussion chuyên sâu

Câu hỏi trừu tượng hơn, liên quan đến topic Part 2. Cần đưa ra quan điểm + giải thích + ví dụ.

Education

Q: Is the education system in your country effective?

That's an interesting question. I'd say it's a mixed bag. On the one hand, Vietnamese students consistently perform well in international assessments in maths and science. On the flip side though, the system tends to prioritize rote learning over critical thinking, which I believe puts students at a disadvantage in the modern workplace.

Q: How can education be improved?

I think the key lies in shifting the focus from exam results to holistic development. This means incorporating more project-based learning, encouraging creativity, and equipping teachers with modern pedagogical methods. Countries like Finland have shown that less testing and more autonomy can actually lead to superior outcomes.

Environment

Q: What environmental problems does your country face?

Well, where do I begin? I'd say the most pressing issue is air pollution, especially in major cities like Hanoi. On top of that, plastic waste remains a significant challenge — Vietnam is actually among the top contributors to ocean plastic globally, which is quite alarming when you think about it.

Society

Q: Do you think social media has more advantages or disadvantages?

It's a double-edged sword, really. Social media has revolutionized the way we communicate and democratized access to information. However, it's also given rise to issues like cyberbullying, misinformation, and declining mental health among young people. On balance, I think the benefits outweigh the drawbacks, provided that users exercise digital literacy.
