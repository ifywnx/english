Writing Task 1

Mô tả quy trình — Process Diagram

Dạng bài Process yêu cầu bạn mô tả các bước tuần tự của một quy trình tự nhiên (natural process) hoặc quy trình sản xuất (man-made process). Điểm then chốt: dùng passive voice và từ nối tuần tự.
ProcessDiagramSequencingPassive
Bí quyết: Đếm số bước trước khi viết — chia đều vào 2 body paragraph.

Từ nối tuần tự

  • Bắt đầu: First, Initially, At the first stage, To begin with
  • Tiếp theo: Then, Next, After that, Subsequently
  • Đồng thời: Meanwhile, At the same time, Simultaneously
  • Kết thúc: Finally, Lastly, In the final stage
  • Kết quả: As a result, Consequently, This leads to

Cấu trúc bài viết

  • Introduction: Paraphrase đề — "The diagram illustrates the process of…"
  • Overview: Tổng số bước + điểm bắt đầu/kết thúc.
  • Body 1: Nửa đầu quy trình.
  • Body 2: Nửa sau quy trình.

Mẫu câu passive

  • "The raw materials are collected and transported to..."
  • "The mixture is then heated to 200 degrees C."
  • "Once cooled, the product is packaged and distributed."
  • "In the next stage, the liquid is filtered through..."
  • "Overall, there are X stages in this process."

Lỗi thường gặp

  • Dùng active voice quá nhiều (nên dùng passive).
  • Không có overview (thiếu tổng số bước).
  • Thiếu từ nối giữa các bước.
  • Sử dụng bullet points thay vì viết thành đoạn.

Bài mẫu band 7+ (Process)

De: The diagram shows how chocolate is produced.

Introduction: The diagram illustrates the process by which chocolate is manufactured, from the initial harvesting of cacao beans to the final product.

Overview: Overall, there are eight stages in the chocolate production process, beginning with the growing of cacao trees and ending with the packaging of the finished chocolate.

Body 1: Initially, cacao trees are grown in tropical regions. Once the pods are ripe, they are harvested and the beans are extracted. The beans are then left to ferment for several days before being dried in the sun.

Body 2: Following this, the dried beans are roasted at high temperatures and then ground into a paste called cacao liquor. Subsequently, sugar and milk are added to the liquor, and the mixture is heated and stirred. Finally, the chocolate is moulded into bars and packaged for distribution.
